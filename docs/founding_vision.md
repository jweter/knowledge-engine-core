# Knowledge Engine

*A Proposal for Accelerating Human Learning, Discovery, and Progress*

Status: This is the project's original founding vision document, preserved
here verbatim as a historical record of intent, not as a spec of what is
currently built or committed. `docs/roadmap.md` and
`docs/roadmap/long_term_vision.md` translate this ambition into a concrete,
buildable phase plan; `docs/phase2_design.md` and the other phase design
docs are where a given slice of it becomes real, scoped, implemented work.
Not everything below is currently scoped -- the Discovery, Education, and
Decision layers described here, for example, are not yet authorized
milestones for any package. This project is deliberately building the
foundation (`knowledge-engine-core`) first: importing and evaluating source
documents well enough that a later, separate reasoning layer has trustworthy
material to work with, rather than attempting the full vision at once.

## Mission Statement

The Knowledge Engine exists to increase humanity's ability to learn,
discover, preserve, and apply knowledge.

Its purpose is to help humanity create new knowledge faster while ensuring
existing knowledge is preserved, traceable, transparent, and continuously
improved.

The long-term objective is to become a living, evolving map of human
understanding.

---

## The Core Problem

Humanity generates knowledge faster than humans can consume it.

Millions of scientific papers, patents, technical reports, books, datasets,
and observations are produced every year.

No individual can read more than a tiny fraction of what exists.

As a result:

- Discoveries remain isolated.
- Knowledge is duplicated.
- Contradictions remain unresolved.
- Expertise is lost when people retire or die.
- Valuable ideas remain undiscovered because no one connected them.

The bottleneck to human progress is no longer information creation.

The bottleneck is information integration.

---

## The Grand Objective

Create a system that continuously:

1. Preserves knowledge.
2. Evaluates evidence.
3. Connects ideas.
4. Identifies contradictions.
5. Generates hypotheses.
6. Suggests experiments.
7. Educates users.
8. Updates itself as new evidence emerges.

The system should function as a continuously evolving extension of
humanity's collective intelligence.

---

## First Principles

### Knowledge Is Never Final

Every claim must remain open to revision.

No statement should be considered permanently true.

Every conclusion exists with a confidence level rather than an absolute
certainty.

### Evidence Matters More Than Authority

Claims should be evaluated based on evidence quality rather than
reputation, institution, status, popularity, or political influence.

### Uncertainty Must Be Visible

The system should explicitly show:

- confidence
- disagreement
- evidence strength
- assumptions
- limitations

The system should never hide uncertainty.

### Contradictions Are Valuable

Disagreement is not a failure.

Contradictions identify opportunities for discovery.

Conflicting evidence should be highlighted and investigated.

### Knowledge Must Be Traceable

Every claim must be linked to:

- source
- author
- publication date
- methodology
- supporting evidence
- contradictory evidence

Users should always be able to trace conclusions to their origins.

### Human Oversight Remains Essential

The Knowledge Engine augments human reasoning.

It does not replace human judgment.

Humans remain responsible for decisions.

## Architecture

### Layer 1 -- Source Vault

**Purpose:** Store humanity's accessible knowledge.

**Sources:**

- scientific papers
- patents
- textbooks
- technical manuals
- public datasets
- government reports
- standards documents
- historical archives

**Goals:**

- preservation
- indexing
- version control
- source verification

**Output:** Raw information repository.

### Layer 2 -- Evidence Engine

**Purpose:** Convert raw information into structured knowledge.

**Functions:**

- extract claims
- identify evidence
- classify methodology
- identify limitations
- estimate evidence quality

**Output:** Evidence records.

### Layer 3 -- Knowledge Graph

**Purpose:** Connect all concepts.

**Functions:**

- relationship mapping
- dependency tracking
- causal links
- contradiction tracking
- domain integration

**Output:** Dynamic map of human knowledge.

### Layer 4 -- Discovery Engine

**Purpose:** Generate new knowledge.

**Functions:**

- identify knowledge gaps
- propose hypotheses
- suggest experiments
- identify overlooked connections
- estimate expected information gain

**Output:** Research opportunities.

### Layer 5 -- Education Engine

**Purpose:** Teach knowledge effectively.

**Functions:**

- adaptive explanations
- personalized learning paths
- prerequisite mapping
- knowledge assessment
- expertise tracking

**Output:** Improved human understanding.

### Layer 6 -- Decision Engine

**Purpose:** Help humans make evidence-based decisions.

**Functions:**

- evidence summaries
- confidence estimates
- scenario modeling
- tradeoff analysis

**Output:** Actionable recommendations.

## Confidence Framework

Every claim receives:

### Evidence Quality Score

Measures:

- study design
- sample size
- replication
- methodology
- statistical rigor

### Consensus Score

Measures:

- agreement among sources
- degree of contradiction

### Stability Score

Measures:

- historical consistency
- frequency of revision

### Recency Score

Measures:

- age of supporting evidence

### Overall Confidence Score

Combined estimate: 0-100%

**Example:**

> Claim: "GLP-1 agonists improve weight loss."
>
> Confidence: 97%
>
> Supporting Sources: 427
>
> Contradicting Sources: 12
>
> Last Updated: July 5, 2026

## Discovery Metrics

The Knowledge Engine should measure:

### Time to Discovery

How quickly new insights are generated.

### Time to Understanding

How quickly users learn new concepts.

### Time to Validation

How quickly hypotheses can be tested.

### Knowledge Coverage

Percentage of known domains represented.

### Contradiction Resolution Rate

How quickly conflicting claims are resolved.

## Long-Term Success Criteria

The project succeeds if:

- Scientific discoveries occur faster.
- Education becomes more effective.
- Human expertise is preserved.
- Contradictions become easier to identify.
- Research duplication decreases.
- Interdisciplinary discoveries increase.
- Evidence-based decision making improves.

---

## Ultimate Vision

The final form of the Knowledge Engine is not a search engine,
encyclopedia, chatbot, or database.

It is a continuously evolving system that helps humanity understand
reality more accurately than any individual, institution, or generation
could alone.

Its purpose is not to replace human intelligence.

Its purpose is to help human intelligence compound across time.

---

## Addendum: Tracking the Unknown

One thing worth adding as a founding principle, and where this idea gets
genuinely interesting:

The Knowledge Engine should not merely store what humanity knows.

It should explicitly track what humanity does not know.

Unknowns, unresolved contradictions, missing experiments, weak evidence
areas, and unanswered questions may become just as important as the
knowledge itself. That feature alone could distinguish it from almost
every existing knowledge system.
